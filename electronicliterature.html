Ear For The Surge

Designed to be heard. A work about rage, inspired by Homeric hexameter and coronavirus. Spoken word, stitched together, woven into layers. Exhibited at ELO 21 COVID-Exhibition, available as a Twitterbot, a YouTube channel, and as a Wordpress Blog.

We Called it Dirt

An open form work, accessed using headphones/speakers and through the web browser. Please type in the letters W, E, C, A, L, L, E, D, I, T, D, I, R, T.

Vocal: John Pat McNamara Text: Claire Fitch Inspiration: an interview between Michael J. Maguire and John Pat McNamara

We Called it Dirt

We never called it clay, we called it dirt. A family anchors into the land. I can see it without having to read it. My life is not an open book, it's electronic literature now. The inside is not the same as outside. I don't know if this is my story or your story. Events in my life were like pebble, stones and big rocks You'd only tell then that you'd know. The guy on the left and the guy on the right. It's gone back to the heather again, like the mountain.

Look (FFEBCD)

Shortlisted for the Hearsay Prize in 2014, Look (FFEBCD). It uses the hexadecimal javascript colour value of blanchedalmond as inspiration for the text that a crowdsourced group of people recorded as spoke, rapped, sung, or vocalized. Using electroacoustic editing techniques, I produced many short audio clips which can be performed via the web browser.
